Part 2: Simulation via numerical integration

The spring constant ks measures the stiffness. With a low ks, the cloth behaves like a jello or slime, very wobbly and stretches a lot when falling. On the other hand, when ks is high, cloth appears to be very stiff, has less wrinkles and folds when fall.

The spring density controls the mass per unit area of the cloth. It affects how strongly it pulls on springs. With low density, the cloth is lightweight, falls slowly. Springs dominate behavior â€” holds its shape more. With high density, cloth is heavy, falls quickly and with force. It stretches the spring more during fall.

The spring dampingcontrols how much energy is lost over time due to things like internal friction or air resistance. With low damping, cloth keeps moving and oscillates a lot. Cloth looks more natural and dynamic. With high damping, cloth motion is quickly suppressed, and cloth is stiffer.
